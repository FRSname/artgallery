Life & Work

Vladislav Raszyk was inspired and guided toward painting by his father Jindřich from childhood. Among the artist's most distinctive motifs was the landscape around the Beskydy Mountains. He also enjoyed painting floral still lifes and figural compositions. He engaged with variations on these themes throughout his entire creative career, utilizing painting techniques ranging from gouache to oil painting and acrylic.

His prestigious recognition for artistic excellence and creative invention was demonstrated, among other things, by his participation in the international exhibition "Bridge Between East and West" in Antwerp, Belgium in 1987. Seventy-five works by artists selected from across Europe were exhibited, including V. Raszyk's painting with a Beskydy theme, which was additionally featured on the representative poster for this project.

The painting traveled with the exhibition to England, the Netherlands, and other significant artistic centers in Europe, after which the work was purchased by a Belgian gallery for its collection.

Exhibitions & Recognition

The artist's work has been presented in many European galleries: Krakow, Katowice, Warsaw, Frankfurt, Cologne, Augsburg, Prague galleries, and countless exhibitions in the region. Many works have found their way into private collections in the USA, Italy, Canada, Norway, Switzerland, France, and England.

Award: Received the "Personality of the Year 2016" award from the city of Český Těšín for long-term contributions to culture.
